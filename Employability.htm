Work Experience

The knowledge I have acquired over the years is not limited to school and university work. During my first year of studying A-levels, I was able to secure a work experience placement at the local charity shop, ‘Mind’. I developed my organisational and time management skills; handling customers, stock and various other tasks. This alien experience taught me the importance of planning ahead as it enabled me to better prepare for unexpected occurrences and handle them adequately. I have participated in several after-school events, including some that were created to provide younger students considering studying A-levels with a better understanding of what to expect from A-level subjects from a student's perspective.

In addition to spending time on both studying and extracurricular activities, I am conscious of the significance of having time to relax. One of my hobbies that I try to make time and pursue is playing football. As a child and still today, I greatly enjoy playing football as it relieves worry of daily stressors. Additionally, it has assisted me in developing leadership traits as it has made me more inclined to adopt a ‘team mindset’.

Career Aspirations

Regarding my personal goals, in spite of the fact that the first-year grade does not count towards the Computer Science degree’s final grade and the pass rate is only 40%, I endeavour to achieve minimum of 70% in the first year as it is well-established that the graduate employment market is competitive. Figures from the HESA (Higher Education Statistic Agency) shows that in 2011, almost 1-in-6 undergraduates at UK universities achieved a first. As a result, attaining high grades throughout the degree’s duration is crucial as it contributes in ‘standing out from the crowd’ (other Computer Science graduates).

In relation to post-graduate job prospects, although I am have not decided upon a definitive occupation I wish to pursue, the Graduate Prospects website provides a wide-ranging selection of Computer Science related professions, e.g. ‘Application Analyst’, ‘Database Administrator’, ‘Games Developer’, ‘Information Systems Manager', ‘IT Consultant’, ‘Web Designer/Developer’, etc.
