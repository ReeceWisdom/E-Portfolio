Self-Introduction

My name is Reece Armani Wisdom, I am 19-years-old (D.O.B: 24/06/1998) and currently studying Computer Science at Manchester Metropolitan University. Although I currently live in a 'Silver En Suite Plus' student accommodation (Wilmslow House IQ) on Oxford Road, Manchester, my previous residence was in Brackenwood, Leeds, where I lived with my Mum & Dad for approximately 18 years.

Why Computer Science?

Computer Science has fascinated me from an early age, which only became more fervent during my GCSE & A-Level studies. Knowledge of the different dimensions of computing is now a significant part of the essential skills required of an educated individual in the 21st century.

It is becoming more and more prevalent in today’s society, modernising the way in which we communicate, work, learn & solve problems; assisting in our day-to-day activities. Constantly exceeding the boundaries of mathematics, science & technology in an effort to advance further. Computer Science is one of the rare professions that is in a continuous state of flux, providing maximum flexibility in the units I study, aspirations and areas of specialisation.

Additionally, the knowledge I have learnt over the two-year Criminology course supports my Computer Science course as the 'Professional Development’ unit covers related areas of professional and legal issues, e.g. freedom of information, data protection legislation, computer misuse, intellectual property rights, e-waste recycling law and computer contracts. Similarly, Philosophy & Ethics aids in my understanding of the Professional Development unit as the overview and comparison of workable ethical theories includes utilitarianism and duty-based theories/ approaches which were both thoroughly scrutinised over the course’s duration.
